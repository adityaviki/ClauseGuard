INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR CONSULTING AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Consulting Agreement ("Agreement") is effective as of September 15, 2025, by and between Vertex Analytics Corp., a Nevada corporation with offices at 400 Innovation Drive, Las Vegas, NV 89101 ("Company"), and DataWise Consulting LLC ("Consultant").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company requires specialized data analytics and machine learning consulting services; and
WHEREAS, Consultant possesses the requisite expertise and is willing to provide such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises herein, the Parties agree as follows:

Section 1. Engagement and Scope of Services

1.1 Company engages Consultant to provide data analytics, machine learning model development, and strategic advisory services as described in Exhibit A (the "Services").

1.2 Consultant shall perform the Services as an independent contractor and not as an employee. Consultant retains full discretion over the manner and means of performing the Services, subject to the project milestones and deliverables set forth in each Statement of Work.

1.3 Consultant shall devote sufficient professional time and attention to ensure timely delivery of all milestones. Consultant may engage subcontractors only with prior written approval of Company.

Section 2. Compensation

2.1 Company shall pay Consultant at the rate of three hundred fifty dollars ($350) per hour for Services rendered, with a monthly cap of eighty thousand dollars ($80,000) unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.2 Consultant shall submit detailed invoices on a bi-weekly basis. Payment shall be due within fifteen (15) business days of receipt of a properly submitted invoice.

2.3 Company shall reimburse Consultant for pre-approved, reasonable business expenses incurred in connection with the Services, subject to Company's expense policy.

Section 3. Intellectual Property

3.1 All work product, inventions, discoveries, designs, algorithms, models, code, reports, and other deliverables created by Consultant in the course of performing Services under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be considered works made for hire and shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company.

3.2 To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, Consultant hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest worldwide in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

3.3 Consultant shall retain ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property, tools, frameworks, and methodologies ("Consultant IP") that exist prior to or are developed independently of this engagement. Consultant grants Company a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use any Consultant IP incorporated into the Work Product.

3.4 Consultant represents and warrants that the Work Product will be original and will not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party.

Section 4. Confidentiality

4.1 Consultant acknowledges that in the course of performing Services, Consultant will have access to and become acquainted with Company's Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" includes, without limitation, proprietary data, algorithms, customer information, business strategies, financial records, pricing models, and any information marked or identified as confidential.

4.2 Consultant shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, without the prior written consent of Company, disclose any Confidential Information to any person or entity, or use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing the Services.

4.3 Upon termination of this Agreement, Consultant shall immediately return or destroy all materials containing Confidential Information and shall certify in writing that all such materials have been returned or destroyed.

4.4 The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Section shall survive the termination of this Agreement indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for a period of five (5) years with respect to all other Confidential Information.

Section 5. Indemnification

5.1 Consultant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company, its parent, subsidiaries, affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns from and against any and all claims, actions, suits, proceedings, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs) arising out of or relating to: (a) any breach of this Agreement by Consultant; (b) Consultant's negligence, gross negligence, or willful misconduct; (c) any claim that the Work Product infringes or misappropriates any third party's intellectual property; or (d) any employment-related claim by Consultant's personnel.

5.2 Company shall indemnify Consultant from claims arising directly from Company's gross negligence or willful misconduct, provided that such claims do not arise from Consultant's breach of this Agreement.

Section 6. Limitation of Liability

6.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, OR ANY OTHER THEORY.

6.2 COMPANY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID TO CONSULTANT UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM. CONSULTANT'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED TWO TIMES (2x) THE AMOUNTS RECEIVED BY CONSULTANT UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

Section 7. Term and Termination

7.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial term of twelve (12) months, unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

7.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other party.

7.3 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving written notice; or (b) becomes the subject of bankruptcy, insolvency, or similar proceedings.

7.4 Upon termination for any reason: (a) Company shall pay Consultant for all Services satisfactorily performed through the effective date of termination; (b) Consultant shall deliver all completed and in-progress Work Product to Company; and (c) the provisions of Sections 3, 4, 5, 6, and 8 shall survive.

Section 8. Governing Law

8.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Nevada, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

8.2 Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Las Vegas, Nevada, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding.

8.3 The prevailing party in any arbitration or court proceeding shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

Section 9. Non-Solicitation

9.1 During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination, neither party shall directly or indirectly solicit for employment any employee or contractor of the other party who was involved in the performance of Services under this Agreement.

Section 10. General Provisions

10.1 Independent Contractor Status. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

10.2 Insurance. Consultant shall maintain professional liability (errors and omissions) insurance with coverage of at least two million dollars ($2,000,000) per occurrence throughout the term of this Agreement.

10.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all Exhibits and Statements of Work, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, agreements, and understandings.

10.4 Amendment. This Agreement may be amended only by a written document signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

10.5 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.6 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email with confirmation of receipt, overnight courier, or certified mail to the addresses set forth on the signature page.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective Date.
